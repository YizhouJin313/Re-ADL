Reasoning-Driven Anomaly Detection and Localization with Image-Level Supervision

Yizhou Jin1,2, Yuezhu Feng1, Jinjin Zhang1, Peng Wang1, Qingjie Liu1,2*, Yunhong Wang1,2*
1State Key Laboratory of Virtual Reality Technology and Systems, Beihang University 2Hangzhou Innovation Institute, Beihang University *Joint corresponding authors
No external vision modules
No dense labels
Reasoning-guided localization

MLLMs inherently contain latent localization cues in reasoning tokens. Can we automatically identify anomaly-relevant tokens (e.g. “scratch”, “mark”) and leverage their attention for pixel-level localization? Can we further activate this intrinsic potential to jointly improve detection, localization, and interpretability under limited supervision?

Abstract

Multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have recently demonstrated remarkable reasoning and perceptual abilities for anomaly detection. However, most approaches remain confined to image-level anomaly detection and textual reasoning, while pixel-level localization still relies on external vision modules and dense annotations. In this work, we activate the intrinsic reasoning potential of MLLMs to perform anomaly detection, pixel-level localization, and interpretable reasoning solely from image-level supervision, without any auxiliary components or pixel-wise labels. Specifically, we propose Reasoning-Driven Anomaly Localization (ReAL), which extracts anomaly-related tokens from the autoregressive reasoning process and aggregates their attention responses to produce pixel-level anomaly maps. We further introduce a Consistency-Guided Reasoning Optimization (CGRO) module that leverages reinforcement learning to align reasoning tokens with visual attentions, resulting in more coherent reasoning and accurate anomaly localization. Extensive experiments on four public benchmarks demonstrate that our method significantly improves anomaly detection, localization, and interpretability. Remarkably, despite relying solely on image-level supervision, our approach achieves performance competitive with MLLM-based methods trained under dense pixel-level supervision.

Key Contributions

  • End-to-end anomaly understanding. ReADL unifies image-level detection, pixel-level localization, and natural-language reasoning without auxiliary vision modules.
  • Reasoning-driven localization. ReAL identifies anomaly-relevant tokens by combining semantic relevance and spatial entropy, then aggregates their visual attention maps.
  • Consistency-guided optimization. CGRO uses reinforcement learning with a reasoning-localization consistency reward to align textual evidence and visual attention.
  • Strong zero-shot generalization. On four public benchmarks, ReADL reaches competitive localization performance despite using only image-level supervision.

Overall Pipeline

Given an input image, the MLLM generates a structured reasoning process and a final anomaly answer. ReAL selects anomaly-related reasoning tokens and aggregates their visual attentions into a pixel-level anomaly map. During training, CGRO reinforces answers that are accurate, well-formed, and visually consistent with the regions described in the reasoning.

Quantitative Results

ReADL is evaluated in a zero-shot setting on MVTec-AD, WFDD, SDD, and DTD. It uses image-level supervision only, yet obtains strong detection, the best average pixel-level AUROC among compared MLLM-based localization systems, and the highest reasoning quality on MVTec-COT.

Visualization Results

ReAL Module Visualization

Not every reasoning token is useful for localization. ReAL filters tokens that are both semantically tied to anomaly concepts and spatially focused, producing sharper maps than naive aggregation over the full reasoning sequence.

CGRO Module Visualization

CGRO reduces reasoning-answer contradictions and concentrates visual attention on true defects. The resulting explanations describe what is anomalous while the heatmaps show where the defect appears.
